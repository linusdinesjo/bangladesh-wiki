Religious Traditions

The main 2 religions in Bangladesh are Islam and Hinduism.4

Muslims

Muslims annually celebrate Ramadan. The celebration lasts for a month and consists of spiritual reflection, self improvement and worship.8

Hindus

Bangladeshi Hindus on the other hand, mainly celebrate Durga Puja,9 the victory of goddess Durga over the demon king Mahishasura. During 10 days the goddess is worshipped in festivals around the nation.10

National Festivities

Pohela Boishakh

Pohela Boishakh Celebration

Pohela Boishakh is the Bangladesh new year's day and it is the most celebrated national festivity in Bangladesh. It consists of family gathering, traditional song and dance, etc.11

Ekushey February

Ekushey February is Bangladesh celebrating the International Mother Language Day, an annually celebrated day promoting awareness of multilingualism. It is celebrated on the 21st of February and pays respect to Bangladeshis who protected their mother tongue with their lives in the year 1952. People attended rallies and several persons died while countless were injured when police opened fire on the rallies. This historical event is said to be a stepping stone for the Bangladesh liberation.12 Many celebrities such as singers and political leaders engage in celebrating this holiday.13
